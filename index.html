The Details

Coding is telling the computer what to do, and is used in fields as varied as arts and design, healthcare and engineering! Interested in what computers can do? Want to get your hands dirty with code? Join us for a weekend to learn just what you, other women, and computers can do. You'll get the opportunity to learn from individuals from one of the best computer science programs offered. And the best part? It's all free, including food!

Who is this for? Female-identifying, non-binary or transgender students in grades 10, 11 or 12 in high school from around the Greater Toronto Area who have little to no coding experience!

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you provide food?
We will provide breakfast, lunch, and snacks on both days.
Does the camp provide housing accommodations?
Unfortunately, HER CODE CAMP is a day camp.
What skills do I need?
Participants should be highly motivated to learn, collaborate, and have a strong background in mathematics.
What is the target age group?
We are looking for participants in grades 10-12 for the 2019/2020 calendar year.
Do I need programming experience?
No prior coding experience is needed.
Do I need to bring my own computer?
No, we got you covered :)

FIRE STARTERS // WHO ARE WE?

We are a group of fierce, female-identifying graduate students at the University of Toronto. Our fields vary from machine learning to bioinformatics to cybersecurity. Our goal is to hack the gender gap and help minority groups grow to their full potential.

Team (from top left to bottom right): Marta Skreta, Heather Gibling, Mariana D'Angelo, Yasaman Rohanifar, Seung-Eun Yi, Joanna Pineda, Brenna Li, Chantal Shaib, Armande Ang Houle, Elyssa Bader

Land Acknowledgement

We wish to acknowledge the land on which the University of Toronto operates. For thousands of years it has been the traditional land of the Huron-Wendat, the Seneca, and most recently, the Mississaugas of the Credit River. Today, this meeting place is still the home to many Indigenous people from across Turtle Island and we are grateful to have the opportunity to work on this land.
